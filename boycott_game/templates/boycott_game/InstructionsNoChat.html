Today, you are participating in an economic decisionâ€‘making study in which a single monopolist sets prices and you as a consumer will decide whether to buy or not. The goal is to understand how consumers may (or may not) coordinate their choices when facing a seller with market power.

  • There will be a monopolist with 5 consumers.
  • In each round, the monopolist sets a price and consumers will decide whether to buy at that price.
  • In all rounds, your earnings depend on your role, price, and buying decision.

    • For Consumers, the goal is to strategize in order to maximize your payoffs to retain more of your value. For reference, this is how to calculate your payoffs:

  • Consumer Payoffs: (Your Value) - (Monopoly Price)

    For the monopolist, your goal is to strategize in order to maximize your payoffs. For reference, this is how to calculate your payoff:

  • Monopolist Payoff: Monopoly Price x Units Sold

    • Individually, the higher the payoff you have, the more money you'll win at the end of the session.
